1. {% trans "What Are Cookies?" %}

{% trans "Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners." %}

{% trans "Cookies contain information that is transferred to your device's hard drive. They help us recognize your device and remember certain information about your visit, such as your preferences and actions on our website." %}

1.1 {% trans "Types of Cookies by Duration" %}
  • {% trans "Session Cookies:" %} {% trans "These are temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They help us track your actions during a single browsing session." %}
  • {% trans "Persistent Cookies:" %} {% trans "These cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. They help us remember your preferences for future visits." %}
1.2 {% trans "First-Party vs Third-Party Cookies" %}
  • {% trans "First-Party Cookies:" %} {% trans "Set directly by our website and can only be read by us." %}
  • {% trans "Third-Party Cookies:" %} {% trans "Set by external services or partners (like analytics or advertising providers) that appear on our website." %}

7. {% trans "Other Tracking Technologies" %}

{% trans "In addition to cookies, we use other tracking technologies:" %}

7.1 {% trans "Web Beacons (Pixels)" %}

{% trans "Small graphic images (also known as 'pixel tags' or 'clear GIFs') embedded in web pages or emails. They work with cookies to track user activity, measure campaign effectiveness, and understand email open rates." %}

7.2 {% trans "Local Storage" %}

{% trans "HTML5 local storage allows websites to store data locally within your browser. Unlike cookies, local storage data doesn't expire and isn't transmitted to the server with every HTTP request. We use local storage to store user preferences and improve application performance." %}

7.3 {% trans "Session Storage" %}

{% trans "Similar to local storage but data is cleared when the browser tab is closed. We use session storage for temporary data during your browsing session." %}

7.4 {% trans "Device Fingerprinting" %}

{% trans "A technique that analyzes various device and browser characteristics to create a unique identifier. We may use limited fingerprinting techniques for security purposes and fraud prevention." %}

7.5 {% trans "ETags" %}

{% trans "HTTP protocol features that allow caching of web resources. While primarily used for performance, they can also be used for tracking. We use ETags primarily for their intended caching purpose." %}

7.6 {% trans "Software Development Kits (SDKs)" %}

{% trans "Third-party SDKs integrated into our mobile applications may collect information about your device and usage. These are governed by the privacy policies of the respective SDK providers." %}
